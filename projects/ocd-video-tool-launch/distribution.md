Higgsfield's virality_predictor was run against the final assembly (job id `fbac9698-34f3-45ae-b74a-433edf30705f`) and returned a hard error: **"Video must be 16 seconds or shorter."** The final cut is 40s (4x10s blocks), so this is a tool constraint, not a transient failure — retrying won't help, since the video's length is fixed and correct per the brief (40s, problem→solution→proof→CTA). No automated hook/retention score is available for this asset.

Given that, here is a manual pre-flight read against the deliverables.md QC notes, standing in for the predictor:
- **Hook (Block 1, "This entire video was made by the tool it's selling."):** This is the brief's designated [STRONGEST] hook — self-referential proof, pattern-interrupt. Deliverables QC flagged it could not visually confirm hook readability in the finished render. **Recommend a human eyes-on check of the first 2 seconds before posting** — if the meta-claim doesn't land instantly and legibly (no burned-in text to reinforce it), this is a re-cut signal, not a caption fix. Caption/on-screen text can support it but cannot rescue a hook that doesn't read on mute in the first frame.
- **Retention risk:** Four fixed 10s blocks with a clean arc (proof → pain → solution → CTA) is structurally sound for the 30s–2min sweet spot the brief cites. The main retention risk is the transition out of Block 1 — if the hook is strong, blocks 2-3 (pain/solution) carry standard mid-video attention decay risk, mitigated by the arc being tight and jargon-light per brief tone notes.
- **CTA (Block 4):** Deliverables QC also flagged it could not confirm CTA cleanliness/legibility. Recommend the same human check on the final 2-3 seconds — CTA text and the OSO-OCD logo lockup need to be crisp since there are no burned-in captions to reinforce "Book a 30-minute consultation."
- **No burned-in captions or on-screen text exist on this asset** — script.md lists optional on-screen text per block, never baked into clips. Each platform section below suggests using platform-native text overlays/covers to carry that support copy, since silent/sound-off viewers otherwise get no reinforcement of the hook, the 82% stat, or the CTA.

## Posting plan
1. **Instagram Reels first** (day 0, e.g. 9-11am local audience time) — this is the brief's primary platform and where the SMB-owner audience actually scrolls; it also lets you catch any hook/CTA legibility issues (flagged above) on the platform that matters most before wider redistribution.
2. **TikTok same day, 2-4 hours after Reels** — same asset, free redistribution; staggering avoids looking like simultaneous mass-posting and lets you fold in any quick overlay-text fix learned from Reels' first engagement signal.
3. **YouTube Shorts next day (day 1)** — Shorts benefits from a distinct, keyword-rich title for search/discovery, and next-day posting avoids diluting Reels' day-0 algorithmic push while still riding the same creative.
4. **LinkedIn last, day 1-2, during a weekday business-hours window (e.g. Tue-Thu, 8-10am)** — B2B feed behavior favors weekday mornings, and LinkedIn's slower, more conversational read benefits from posting after the short-form platforms have already validated the hook.

This spacing lets the team react to any human eyes-on hook/CTA issues (see pre-flight note) before the asset reaches LinkedIn's more scrutinizing B2B buyer-intent audience, while still shipping all four platforms within ~48 hours to capture the launch news cycle.
