# Creative Brief: Sleepless Nights (OSO Operational Systems Optimization)

- **Goal:** lead-generation *(locked by intake)*

- **Audience:** Blue-collar field-service business owners — oilfield/trades-style operations with trucks, crews, and job sites — who mastered the trade itself but are now drowning in the back-office side (payroll, invoicing, compliance, tech) nobody ever trained them to run.

- **Core promise:** You don't have to carry the operational chaos alone — OSO can't promise to fix everything overnight, but we will help surface the problems keeping you up at night and address them, starting with a free 30-minute consultation.

- **Hook (first 2 seconds):**
  1. **[STRONGEST]** Extreme close-up on a bedside clock flipping to 3:14 AM; pull back to an exhausted owner staring at the ceiling in the dark, phone glowing beside them. On-screen text: "3:14 AM. Payroll's due Friday." — visceral, specific, immediately says "this is about me" before a single word of narration.
  2. Phone on the nightstand buzzing in the dark, screen stacking up unread invoice reminders and missed calls in rapid-fire — visual chaos standing in for the mental chaos of the sleepless owner.
  3. OSO Grizzly silhouette standing at the foot of the bed like a guardian in the dark, staring at the sleepless owner — striking, but introduces the mascot before the pain is established, so it risks feeling like a gimmick rather than a gut-punch. Held in reserve for beat 3.

- **Message arc:** *(4 beats → 4 blocks at 40s; final block doubles as CTA + reserved logo end-card per standing instruction)*
  1. **Hook (0-10s):** 3:14 AM. The owner is awake, alone with the weight of the business — payroll's due, the mind won't shut off.
  2. **Problem montage (10-20s):** Rapid-fire relatable checklist of what's actually running through their head — customers not paying on time, invoices stuck in the backlog, trucks still out on the road, a crew member not doing what they're supposed to. Sensory, fast-cut, no exaggeration needed.
  3. **Honest reframe (20-30s):** The turn — "We can't promise we can fix everything. But we can help bring the problems to the surface and address them." Paired with the emotional core: you learned the work extremely well; nobody taught you the finances, the accounting, the compliance, the technology. OSO Grizzly enters here as the steady, competent presence — not a savior, a partner.
  4. **CTA + logo (30-40s):** "You don't have to do it alone. OSO can help." Direct to the free 30-minute consultation. Final seconds reserved for the OSO shield end-card (handled in post).

- **CTA:** "Book a 30-minute Consultation" — emphasize it's **free**, no pressure, no long pitch. *(Locked by intake; recommend this becomes the standing lead-generation CTA in `brand/brand-profile.md`, currently TBD.)*

- **Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts — 9:16 vertical (all locked). No secondary platform for this cut; LinkedIn/16:9 could be a future re-edit but is out of scope here.

- **Length:** 40 seconds (4 blocks × 10s). Chosen over 30s because the arc needs a full beat for the problem montage (payroll, late-paying customers, invoicing, vehicles, crew accountability) to feel earned and specific rather than rushed, plus a distinct honest-reframe beat before the CTA — compressing to 3 beats/30s would make the "we can't promise everything, but..." line feel tacked-on rather than the emotional turn it needs to be.

- **Tone notes:** Confident, per brand voice — but confidence here means *calm authority*, not hype. This is a quiet, 3 AM kind of ad: minimal narration, let the visuals of exhaustion and clutter do the work, then land the reframe with warmth and directness. No fear-mongering, no dramatized crisis beyond what any owner in this world would recognize instantly. The OSO Grizzly should read as a steady, protective presence — competent backup, not a rescuer swooping in. Close on the brand's confident register ("Smarter Workflows. Stronger Bottom Line." spirit) without over-promising results the consultation hasn't yet earned.

- **Proof points / facts used:** None — this brief is built entirely from the user's own experiential claims about owner life (payroll stress, late-paying customers, invoicing delays, vehicles on the road, crew accountability, sleepless nights). No statistics were invented or sourced; all pain points are treated as lived-experience relatability, not measurable claims, and should stay that way in execution.

- **Risks:**
  - Pain-point-led ads can tip into feeling exploitative or manipulative if the problem montage lingers too long without relief — keep beat 2 fast and matter-of-fact, not maudlin.
  - The "we can't promise we can fix everything" honesty line is a credibility asset but must not be undercut by an over-confident CTA — the script/voiceover should keep that humility intact right up to the ask.
  - Depicting blue-collar/trades owners risks caricature if visuals lean stereotypical — reference imagery should feel specific and respectful (real gear, real trucks, real exhaustion), not generic "hard hat" shorthand.
  - Compliance/finance/accounting claims must stay in "we help you see the problem" territory, not imply OSO guarantees regulatory or financial outcomes it hasn't assessed yet.
